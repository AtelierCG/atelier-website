# Atelier — Website Development Brief
**Version 1.1 | May 2026**
**Prepared for: Christian Grancourt, Founder**

## 1. Brand Strategy Overview

### The Core Premise

Atelier is not an AI agency. It is not a consultancy. It is not a software house. It is a **philosophy made operational** — the conviction that technology and human beings are seeking the same three things: connectivity, integration, and flow.

This positioning is rare because it is genuinely rare. The founder holds a 30-year synthesis of enterprise systems architecture, organisational development, Appreciative Inquiry, and personal transformation practice. That synthesis is Atelier's most valuable asset, and the website must communicate it without reducing it to bullet points or corporate language.

The website must do one thing above all else: **make an aligned visitor feel immediately recognised.** They should land on the page and think "this is the thing I didn't know I was looking for."

### Competitive Differentiation

The market is saturated with two types of AI brands: the technical-aggressive ("we build faster, smarter, cheaper") and the human-washing ("AI with heart"). Atelier occupies a third position that neither inhabits — a studio founded in a genuine philosophical synthesis, with the technical depth to build serious systems and the human depth to understand what those systems are actually for.

The website's tone must never slide toward either of the two saturated modes. It is calm, precise, and visionary. It earns trust through clarity of thought, not through claims.

### The AI² Concept

The Artificial Intelligence + Appreciative Inquiry duality is Atelier's most intellectually distinctive asset. It should be present on the site as a discoverable layer — not front-and-centre on the hero (that would be too academic), but woven into the philosophy section and referenced in a way that rewards attentive visitors. The superscript "²" in AI² is a visual signature that can appear as a subtle design element.

## 2. Website Positioning Statement

**For visionary founders, transformation leaders, conscious organisations, and anyone working at the frontier of human and societal potential — Atelier is the studio that builds at the intersection of technology and what it means to be human.**

Unlike generic AI agencies that separate technical execution from human meaning, Atelier was founded on the belief that both artificial intelligence and human beings are ultimately seeking connectivity, integration, and flow. That belief operates at every scale — individual, organisational, societal, global. Every system Atelier builds serves that convergence.

**Important framing note for all copy and content decisions:** Atelier's scope is not limited to organisations. The founder's graduate work spans Positive Organisational Development and Change *and* Positive Business and Societal Change. This is a studio with civilisational ambition — working with individuals, communities, organisations, and systems at whatever scale the work demands. The word "organisational" should never dominate the site's language. Humanity is the frame. Organisations are one context within it.

## 3. Messaging Strategy

### Message Hierarchy

### Target Audience Framing

Atelier serves at every scale. The audience is not "organisations" — it is anyone operating at the intersection of technology, human potential, and meaningful change. This includes:

- Individuals seeking technology that serves their growth and purpose
- Founders building companies with a genuine sense of mission
- Leaders driving transformation within organisations or across sectors
- Social innovators and impact-driven initiatives
- Governments, NGOs, or civic institutions exploring technology's role in societal change
- Anyone who senses that technology and human flourishing are not in opposition

The site's language should speak to a person, not a procurement committee.

> *"PURPOSE-DRIVEN TECHNOLOGY THAT SERVES HUMANITY"*
> *"AI-DRIVEN DEVELOPMENT STUDIO"*

### Voice Principles

- **Visionary without being vague.** Ground philosophical ideas in concrete examples.
- **Confident without being loud.** The brand does not need to shout. It knows what it is.
- **Human without being sentimental.** Warmth comes from clarity and recognition, not from emojis and exclamation marks.
- **Never use:** "leverage," "synergy," "cutting-edge," "game-changing," "disruptive," "innovative," or any AI startup cliché.

### Suggested Homepage Headline Options

1. *"Where technology and humanity find flow."*
2. *"Built at the intersection of intelligence and purpose."*
3. *"Technology that knows what it's for."*
4. *"Systems that serve. Technology that understands."*

Recommendation: Option 1 or 3. Final copy to be refined with the founder. The headline should be short (under 8 words), carry the philosophy without explaining it, and feel immediately resonant to the target audience.

## 4. Emotional Tone and Voice

The website should evoke the feeling of: **entering a space where serious thinking has already happened.** The visitor senses that whoever built this has thought more deeply about the questions they care about than most people they've encountered.

Emotional registers to sustain throughout:

- **Calm authority** — the confidence of someone who doesn't need to prove themselves
- **Intellectual warmth** — curiosity and depth, not coldness
- **Quiet aspiration** — inspiring without being motivational-poster
- **Respectful precision** — honouring the visitor's intelligence

What to avoid emotionally:
- Hype or urgency (no countdown timers, no "limited spots available")
- False intimacy (no "Hey, I'm Christian!" casualness on an enterprise-facing site)
- Corporate distance (no third-person boilerplate)
- Spiritual vagueness (the philosophy is grounded and structured, not woo)

## 5. Visual Design Direction

### Foundation: The Brand Identity

The existing visual identity establishes the complete design DNA. The three-curve orbital logo — a luminous sphere at centre, three flowing arcs in circuit-board blue/cyan, glowing node points — encodes the connectivity/integration/flow philosophy visually. Every design decision on the website should feel like an extension of this mark.

**Do not compromise or simplify the visual identity for the web.** The sophistication of the logo is the benchmark for the site's visual ambition.

### Colour Palette (Extracted from Brand Assets)

| Role | Value | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|
| Background primary | `#060B1E` | Deep space navy — main page background |
| Background secondary | `#0A1230` | Slightly lighter navy — card/section backgrounds |
| Background surface | `#0D1640` | Elevated surfaces, nav background |
| Accent cyan | `#00D4FF` | Primary glow, active states, key highlights |
| Accent electric blue | `#0088FF` | Secondary accent, links, interactive elements |
| Accent violet | `#7B2FFF` | Tertiary accent, gradient terminus, depth |
| Text primary | `#FFFFFF` | Headings |
| Text secondary | `#B8C8E8` | Body copy, descriptive text |
| Text muted | `#5A7099` | Labels, captions, metadata |
| Gradient — main | `linear-gradient(135deg, #00D4FF 0%, #0066FF 50%, #7B2FFF 100%)` | Logo gradient, hero accents, CTA buttons |
| Gradient — subtle | `linear-gradient(180deg, #00D4FF22 0%, transparent 100%)` | Glows, section dividers |

The gradient transitions from cyan → electric blue → violet. This is the brand's chromatic signature and should appear consistently: in the logo, in the hero glow, in interactive button states, in section accents.

### Typography

**Display / Heading font:** A geometric sans-serif with clean, wide proportions and high legibility at large sizes. The existing ATELIER wordmark uses wide letter-spacing and a geometric construction.

Recommended options (Google Fonts, free for commercial use):
- **Syne** (primary recommendation) — geometric, architectural, distinctive, premium feel
- **Outfit** (alternative) — clean and technical with personality
- **Space Grotesk** (backup) — technically strong but becoming somewhat common

Use Syne for all headings (H1–H3). Apply `letter-spacing: 0.05em` to 0.12em on uppercase display text to echo the wordmark's spaced treatment.

**Body copy font:** A humanist sans-serif that reads cleanly at smaller sizes and carries warmth.
- **DM Sans** (primary recommendation) — excellent legibility, subtle warmth, pairs well with geometric display fonts
- **Inter** (fallback only — widely used, less distinctive)

**Monospace (for AI² and technical references):**
- **DM Mono** or **JetBrains Mono** — use sparingly for code snippets, technical labels, or the AI² notation

**Type Scale:**
- Display / Hero: 64–96px, Syne, weight 600–700, letter-spacing 0.08em
- H1: 48–56px, Syne, weight 600
- H2: 32–40px, Syne, weight 500
- H3: 22–28px, Syne or DM Sans, weight 500
- Body: 16–18px, DM Sans, weight 400, line-height 1.7
- Caption / Label: 12–13px, DM Sans or DM Mono, weight 400–500, letter-spacing 0.1em

## 6. UX / UI Direction

### Design Principles

**Premium minimalism with depth.** The page should feel spacious and considered. Negative space is used deliberately — not as emptiness but as breathing room that gives each element weight.

**Motion as meaning.** Animations should serve the content. The orbital logo's flowing curves suggest motion and flow — this kinetic quality should translate into subtle page animations that reinforce the brand's ideas, not distract from them.

**Immersive but accessible.** The visual depth of the dark theme and glowing accents should be achieved without creating accessibility problems. Text contrast ratios must meet WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for body text). The visual sophistication should not come at the cost of legibility.

**Desktop-first in design, mobile-first in development.** The target audience (enterprise leaders, visionary founders, transformation executives) will often first encounter Atelier on desktop. Design the primary experience for widescreen, then adapt carefully for mobile. Do not let mobile constraints dictate the desktop vision.

### Layout Principles

- Max content width: 1200px, centred
- Hero section: full viewport height (100vh) — the logo and headline deserve this space
- Section padding: generous — 120px vertical minimum on desktop, 64px on mobile
- Grid: 12-column grid with 24px gutters on desktop; single column with 24px side padding on mobile
- Cards / panels: glass-morphism style — semi-transparent dark background with subtle border and glow

### Interaction States

- **Links:** Cyan underline on hover, smooth 200ms transition
- **Buttons:** Gradient background (cyan to violet), subtle glow on hover (box-shadow spread), slight scale-up (1.02×) on hover
- **Cards:** Subtle border-glow on hover (0 0 24px rgba(0, 212, 255, 0.15))
- **Navigation:** Blur-backdrop nav bar that becomes more opaque on scroll

- Full viewport height
- Animated background: slow-moving particle field or animated orbital rings echoing the logo (CSS or lightweight Three.js / Canvas — keep under 200kb total JS)
- Logo mark centred or upper-left depending on art direction choice

---

### Section 2 — The Philosophy
**Purpose:** Establish the depth and distinctiveness of the intellectual foundation.

Content: The connectivity / integration / flow framework, expressed in one well-crafted paragraph or three short parallel statements. This is where the AI² concept can be hinted at — perhaps a subtle footnote or typographic treatment of "AI²" that invites curiosity.

Design: Wide, centred text block. Large pull-quote treatment. Possibly a subtle animated element (slowly rotating orbital arcs). No images. The words should be the visual event.

---

### Section 3 — What We Build
**Purpose:** Credibility through specificity.

Three to four capability areas, each expressed as a heading + 2–3 sentence description. These are not service offerings listed for a brochure — they are categories of problem Atelier solves.

Suggested capability areas (to be confirmed with founder):
- **AI-Driven Systems** — custom AI product development, agent architectures, intelligent automation
- **Platform & Product Architecture** — full-stack systems design, database architecture, API ecosystems
- **Human & Societal Transformation** — systems thinking and Appreciative Inquiry applied to change at any scale: individual, organisational, community, societal
- **Purpose-Driven Innovation** — technology strategy in service of meaningful outcomes — for founders, leaders, and initiatives working at the frontier of what's possible

Design: Icon-free. Text-led. Cards with subtle glassmorphism. Grid of 2×2 on desktop, single column on mobile.

---

### Section 4 — Selected Work (sparse in V1)
**Purpose:** Social proof without case-study bloat.

In Version 1, this can be minimal: 1–3 projects named with a one-line descriptor and (if permissible) client sector. No full case studies yet. This section can be a holding pattern that expands over time.

Alternatively, if no client work can be shown yet, replace with a positioning statement section instead — and add work later.

---

### Section 5 — The Intersection
**Purpose:** The emotional crescendo of the page — where technology and humanity meet.

This is the most distinctively Atelier section. It should express, visually and through copy, the core synthesis: that advanced technology and deep human development are not opposites — they are seeking the same things.

Design: A visual metaphor element (the orbital/flow animation echoing the logo) with text overlay. Possibly the three words — CONNECTIVITY · INTEGRATION · FLOW — displayed as a typographic centrepiece with subtle animation. This section should feel like the philosophical heart of the site.

---

### Section 6 — Contact / Invitation
**Purpose:** Quiet, confident invitation to connect.

Not a "Request a Proposal" form. A simple, elegant contact section with a brief paragraph and either:
a) A single email link
b) A minimal form (name, email, message — nothing more)

Suggested copy direction: *"If what you've read resonates — if you're building something that matters — we'd like to hear about it."*

No phone number unless specifically desired. No social media links unless accounts are active.

## 9. Content Hierarchy

The homepage content should follow this emotional arc:

1. **Arrival** — brand impression (logo, headline)
2. **Recognition** — "this is different" (philosophy)
3. **Comprehension** — "this is what they do" (capabilities)
4. **Evidence** — "this is real" (work)
5. **Resonance** — "this is why it matters" (intersection)
6. **Connection** — "I want to engage" (contact)

Each section earns the next. A visitor who doesn't connect at step 2 will not convert — and that is fine. The site is not optimised for high-volume lead conversion. It is optimised for attracting deeply aligned clients.

## 11. Motion and Animation Direction

### Guiding Principle
Animation should feel like the natural behaviour of a system in flow. Nothing should feel sudden, gratuitous, or performative.

### Recommended Animations

**Hero background:** Slowly orbiting particle field — nodes connected by thin luminous lines, moving at 0.1–0.3× normal speed. Reminiscent of the circuit-point motif in the logo. Implemented in Canvas or lightweight WebGL. Strictly performance-budgeted: 60fps on a 3-year-old MacBook.

**Logo entrance:** The three-curve logo animates in on page load — arcs drawing in sequence (stroke-dashoffset animation), then the central sphere pulses once with a soft glow. Duration: 1.2s total.

**Section reveals:** Elements fade up and in on scroll entry (`IntersectionObserver`). Translate from 20px below, opacity 0→1, duration 400ms, ease-out. Stagger child elements by 80ms.

**Gradient text:** The brand tagline uses a slowly-shifting gradient animation (background-position keyframes), creating a gentle shimmer effect that reinforces the flow concept.

**Card hover:** On hovering capability cards, a subtle border-glow intensifies (box-shadow transition, 200ms ease). No scale transforms on content cards — reserve scale for buttons only.

**Cursor:** Consider a custom cursor with a small trailing glow dot on desktop — this is a distinctive touch used by premium studios. Must degrade gracefully (hidden on touch devices).

### What to Avoid
- Parallax scrolling on text elements (reduces legibility)
- Autoplay video in hero (bandwidth, performance, distraction)
- Rapid or complex keyframe animations (they suggest restlessness, not flow)
- Scroll-jacking (never hijack the browser's scroll behaviour)

## 12. Mobile-First Considerations

Despite designing for desktop primary experience, the build must be fully responsive.

**Navigation:** Hamburger menu on mobile, full slide-in panel with the same glassmorphism aesthetic.

**Hero:** Reduce headline to 36–42px on mobile. Logo mark only (no brand descriptor text). Keep the animated background but reduce particle count by 60%.

**Typography:** Body text stays at 16px minimum on mobile (never scale down for density). Line length: 65–75 characters maximum on any viewport.

**Cards:** Full-width single column, with 24px horizontal padding. Retain the glassmorphism borders and glow.

**Spacing:** 64px section padding on mobile (down from 120px desktop). Never let sections feel cramped — the premium feel depends on breathing room.

**Touch interactions:** No hover-dependent functionality. All interactive states accessible via tap.

**Performance:** Target < 3s First Contentful Paint on mobile 4G. Lazy-load all images below the fold. Use `next/image` for automatic optimisation. Limit third-party scripts to PostHog and Sentry only.

## 14. Immersive Interaction Suggestions

These are optional enhancements for Version 1.1+ once the core site is stable:

**Magnetic cursor elements:** Large headings and CTA buttons subtly attract the cursor on hover (JavaScript mouse-tracking, CSS transform). Creates a premium, tactile feel.

**Text scramble reveal:** Section headings reveal using a character-scramble animation (cycling through random characters before settling on the correct letter). Used sparingly — perhaps on the philosophy section heading only.

**The AI² Easter egg:** Somewhere on the site (perhaps the footer, or the about page), hovering over "AI²" reveals a tooltip or inline expansion that briefly explains the Artificial Intelligence + Appreciative Inquiry duality. This rewards attentive visitors without cluttering the main experience.

**Orbital cursor trail:** On the hero section only, the cursor leaves a faint luminous trail that fades over 800ms, echoing the orbital arcs of the logo.

**Smooth page transitions:** Using Framer Motion's `AnimatePresence`, pages slide or fade between each other rather than hard-cutting. Duration: 300ms.

## 15. SEO Considerations

The site is not primarily SEO-optimised for high search volume — it is positioning-led and relationship-driven. However, basic SEO hygiene is non-negotiable.

**On-page:**
- `<title>` tags: `Atelier — AI-Driven Development Studio` for homepage; page-specific titles for inner pages
- `<meta description>`: 150–160 chars per page, written as positioning statements not keyword dumps
- Open Graph tags for all pages (for LinkedIn sharing, which is the primary social channel for the target audience)
- Semantic HTML: proper heading hierarchy, landmark elements (`<main>`, `<nav>`, `<footer>`)
- Alt text on all images including the logo (treat it as meaningful content)

**Structured data:**
- `Organization` schema on homepage
- `Person` schema on the about page (founder)

**Technical:**
- `robots.txt` and `sitemap.xml` auto-generated by Next.js
- No pages should be `noindex` except `/thank-you` (post-form submission)
- All pages served over HTTPS (Vercel default)
- Core Web Vitals target: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, FID < 100ms

**Keywords to target (long-tail, positioning-led):**
- "purpose-driven AI development studio"
- "AI systems and human development"
- "technology and organisational transformation"
- "conscious technology studio"
- "AI for social impact"

## 16. Future Scalability Considerations

The site architecture should accommodate these additions without major restructuring:

**Blog / Thinking section:** Next.js MDX or a headless CMS (Sanity or Contentlayer). Add when the founder has 3+ articles ready to publish. Do not create an empty blog.

**Project case studies:** A `[slug]` dynamic route under `/work/[slug]`. Template the component now; populate content later.

**Payments / Stripe:** If Atelier introduces productised services or workshops, Stripe Connect (already in the confirmed stack) integrates cleanly via API routes.

## 17. Balancing Technology and Humanity Visually

This is the central visual challenge of the project — and the most important to get right.

**The risk:** A visually sophisticated dark tech aesthetic can easily feel cold, masculine, or alienating. It can signal "for engineers only" when the target audience includes transformation leaders, executives, and people drawn to the human development dimension of Atelier.

**The resolution — three design choices:**

1. **Typography warmth:** The choice of DM Sans for body copy is deliberate. It has a humanist character that softens the geometric coldness of the display font. The contrast between Syne (architectural, precise) and DM Sans (warm, readable) enacts the technology/humanity duality at the typographic level.

2. **Spatial generosity:** Premium dark tech sites often feel cramped and intense. Atelier's site should feel expansive. Wide margins, generous line height, unhurried pacing between sections — these communicate that the studio is not rushed or anxious.

3. **Language as the human layer:** The copy is where humanity most directly enters. The words should carry warmth, recognition, and philosophical depth. If the visuals suggest intelligence, the words should suggest wisdom.

**The unicorn:** On the `/about` page, the unicorn symbol can appear as a personally meaningful emblem — ideally at a modest scale (not a full-bleed banner) and introduced with the founder's own language around what it means. It should feel like discovering something personal about the founder, not a branded mascot.

## 18. How the AI² Philosophy Appears Throughout the Experience

This concept should not be explained on the homepage. It should be discoverable.

**Suggested touchpoints:**

- **Footer:** A small "AI²" mark in the footer — monospace font, no explanation. Curious visitors will ask.
- **About page:** Full explanation of both dimensions (Artificial Intelligence and Appreciative Inquiry), written as a personal founding story.
- **Meta / Open Graph:** The `og:description` could subtly reference it: "At the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Appreciative Inquiry."
- **Loading / Splash animation:** If the site uses a brief loader on first visit, the AI² mark could appear during that moment.
- **Hover tooltip:** As noted above, hovering "AI²" in the footer triggers a brief inline explanation.
- **Section headings:** The philosophy section could use the heading "AI² — What Becomes Possible?" as a way to introduce it to engaged readers.

The principle: **reward attention.** The AI² concept is not marketing copy — it is the founding insight of the studio. It belongs to the visitor who takes the time to look.
